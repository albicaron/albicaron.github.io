About Me

Welcome! I am Alberto Caron, a Senior Research Associate at The Alan Turing Institute. Previously I was a PhD student at the Department of Statistical Science, University Collge London. Before that, I graduated with a Master of Science in Statistics & Econometrics from the Bocconi University. My research very generally revolves around Causality and Bayesian/Probabilistic Machine Learning methods, and how these can be harnessed for decision-making.

Previous to, but also during, my PhD, I have worked on a number of external applied research/consultancy projects on environmental and energy policy evaluation. Particularly, I have served as a researcher/consultant for the World Bank Group and for the UK governmental Department of Business, Energy & Industrial Strategy.
